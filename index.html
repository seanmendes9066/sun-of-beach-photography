ABOUT MY GEAR

It started with a simple, secondhand DSLR — a gift from a friend back in college. A true "legacy" that lacks even the luxury of autofocus.

I have to manually dial in the focus to capture every fleeting moment. It’s slow, it’s intentional, and every photo carries a weight of sentimental value that no high-end gear could ever replace.

"You don't need a high-end camera to find happiness in photography;
you just need the one that speaks to your soul."
